Nothing here changes who can see or do what — only where you go to do it.

What changed based on ClickUp

Stage is now separate from Status. Status stays the simple lifecycle you already use — Not Started, In Progress, Waiting Reply, Submitted, Completed, Cancelled — and still drives the board columns and the accountability workflow. Stage is new: an optional, free-text pipeline label for tasks that move through steps of their own, like the receivable chain's "Submitted to Civil Dept → Back to FD Dept → Payment Released". Most tasks won't need one; it only shows up when a department sets one.

Completion date is now tracked automatically. Today the database only has a due date and a created date — there's no record of when a task was actually finished. The rebuild adds a completion timestamp, stamped the moment a task is marked Completed, so completed cards show "Completed 09/07" instead of just a due date. That also unlocks reporting you don't have yet, like average days-late-to-close by department.

Colours are now pulled from your actual component styles — Status and Priority pills use the exact colour pairings from StatusBadge / PriorityBadge in SharedUI.tsx, and the company tags (UTPL blue, IFPL green, Baranh amber, Haute Dolci purple) are the same badge colours already used on your Folder-it page, not new ones I invented.

Two ways into subtasks now, on the two wired-up examples (A&R Hotels in List view, and PESCO/MEPCO in both List and Board). Click directly on the task title or card and the full detail panel opens — description, metadata, subtasks, comments, all in one place. Click the small caret (▸ in List, "Subtasks" in Board) instead, and a compact tick-box checklist expands right there on the main screen, with its own "+ add subtask" line — no need to open anything for a quick tick-off, and the "x/y" count next to the task updates as you go.

The "Mark task complete" button only lives in the full panel, since that's a formal status change — tick every box (inline or in the panel) and it unlocks. Tasks with no subtasks skip the lock entirely. One honest caveat: in this static mockup the inline checklist and the full-panel checklist are separate demo copies, so ticking one won't move the other here — in the real build they'd read from the same underlying list and always match.

Company and department are now required tags, not optional fields. Try the quick-add bar above — the "+ Add" button stays greyed out until you've picked both a company tag and a department tag; the hint underneath tells you why. I kept them as two dropdowns rather than a fully custom tag-picker, because a dropdown that must be filled behaves exactly like a compulsory tag in practice and is much less to build — but colours it like the company badges the moment you pick one, so it still reads as tagging, not paperwork. If you'd rather have a genuinely free-form tag picker (type-to-search, add new tags on the fly), that's a bigger build — happy to do it, just tell me.

Issue date sits next to due date now, in List view and My Tasks (Board stays due-date-only, on purpose, since you asked for smaller cards) — see the "Issued 02/07 → Due 04/07" line on each row.

New Team tab answers "which employee is on time and which isn't" directly — see it above.
